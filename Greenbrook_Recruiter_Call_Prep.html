Recruiter Screen · Prep Sheet

Greenbrook Medical — VP of Finance

First-round recruiter call · keep it warm, fit-focused, not technical

Resolve early: remote-eligible states are FL, VA, NY, NJ, MO, TX — Colorado isn't listed. Raise the location question in the screen so it's not a surprise later.

1 · Your 60-second walkthrough

"I'm a healthcare finance leader with 12+ years, and I've spent the last seven building and running finance at venture-backed care-delivery companies. I started as a CPA in audit at BDO, then did consolidated reporting for a $2.5B health system at Intermountain — so I have the accounting and controls foundation. From there I moved into strategic finance in value-based and primary care: at VillageMD I was Finance Manager for Value-Based Care, forecasting contract performance and modeling revenue and cost drivers; at Sprinter Health, a Series A, I ran finance, accounting, and revenue cycle hands-on and managed runway through a tight period; and at Connections Health Solutions, a Series B, I own end-to-end FP&A — I rebuilt budgeting on Adaptive Planning, built the five-year model behind fundraising, and drove 15%+ center-level profitability. I also build AI-enabled dashboards into the workflow. My sweet spot is being the hands-on finance leader who stands up the function in a scaling, value-based business."

Hits the arc: CPA foundation → VBC fluency → build-from-scratch → fundraising → modern/AI. Keep to a minute.

2 · Why you're looking

"I've effectively been the senior-most finance person at venture scale, and I'm ready to step fully into that VP seat — to be the first finance leader who builds the function from the ground up rather than inheriting one. This role is the exact intersection of what I've done: value-based senior-care economics from VillageMD, and building finance from scratch at Connections and Sprinter. And I respect how Greenbrook got here — bootstrapped, outcomes-first, proving the model before raising. I want to help scale and professionalize that foundation."

Frame it entirely as moving toward ownership and mission — never criticize your current employer.

3 · Questions to ask the recruiter (pick 3–4)

  1. "What's the interview process from here — how many rounds, who would I meet, and the rough timeline?" (You know there's a case study + values interview — nodding to that is a plus.)
  2. "What's the story behind this role opening now? It reads like the first dedicated finance leader — is that right, and what's driving the timing?"
  3. "What does the CEO most want this person to accomplish in the first year — what would make this a great hire in their eyes?"
  4. "How would you describe the culture, and what kind of person really thrives here?" (They're values-driven: Heart, Excellence, Accountability, Resilience, Teamwork.)
  5. "Can we talk through location expectations? I'm in Denver and saw it's NYC-preferred with remote in a few states — I want to make sure that's not a blocker before we both invest time."
  6. "Is there anything in my background you'd want me to address or expand on as we move forward?" (Strong closer — invites concerns now.)

Lead with #1 and #5 — process and location are what a recruiter answers best.

4 · Be ready for these — drafted answers

"Tell me about yourself."

Use the 60-second open above. One minute, then stop.

"Why this role / why Greenbrook?"

"It's the intersection of the two things I've done — value-based senior-care economics from VillageMD, and building a finance function from the ground up at Connections and Sprinter. And I respect how Greenbrook got here: bootstrapped, outcomes-first, proving the model before raising. I want to help take that foundation and scale it as the first finance leader."

"Why are you looking to leave?"

"I've been operating as the senior-most finance person at venture scale, and I'm ready to step fully into the VP seat — to build the function as the first finance hire rather than inherit one. It's about moving toward more ownership and a mission I connect with, not away from anything."

"What are your salary expectations?"

"I'm targeting a base in the [your number — e.g., $215–250K] range plus bonus and equity, but I'm flexible on the mix and most focused on the overall opportunity and the equity story at this stage. Where does the range land for this role?"

Give a range, then let them reveal their band. Don't anchor low.

"What's your availability / are you interviewing elsewhere?"

"I'm actively exploring a few opportunities, but Greenbrook is one I'm genuinely excited about. I can start with reasonable notice and I'm happy to move quickly through your process."

"What do you know about us?"

"Senior-focused primary care in Tampa Bay, founded by two brothers carrying on their father's Medicare Advantage practice since '91, bootstrapped, with a Tampa General joint venture — and a value-based model built around outcomes rather than chasing risk scores."

This level of specificity stands out in a screen.

Tone reminder: a recruiter screen is as much about energy and fit as substance. Warm, conversational, concise. You've got the background — let it show.
